My Projects – A Journey of Hard Work

Behind every project in my portfolio, there’s a story of effort, late nights, struggles, and small victories that kept me moving forward.

Major Projects

  • Chakwal Climate Analysis: Explored 24 years of climate and solar trends. Not just numbers, but meaningful insights about my hometown.
  • Solving Mysteries with Code: A sentiment analysis project analyzing reviews of seven mystery games. Learned how coding and emotions intersect.
  • PSL Commentary & Historical Analysis: Handled ball-by-ball cricket data from 2016 to 2025 in R. Built an interactive Shiny dashboard after hours of cleaning and structuring data.
  • Global Superstore Project: Used SQL and Python to explore sales, regions, and profit patterns. Learned discipline and advanced problem-solving skills.

Across all these projects, I pushed myself to build dashboards in Tableau, Streamlit, Shiny, and Dash by Plotly. Sometimes the code didn’t run. Sometimes the visualization wouldn’t render. And honestly, there were days I felt too stressed to continue.

But persistence always won over frustration. Step by step, I fixed bugs, learned new libraries, and refined my work until everything finally clicked.

Looking back, these projects aren’t just about technical skills. They are proof of my hard work, my ability to adapt, and my determination to finish what I start. Each project carries a piece of my journey — and together, they tell the story of a data analyst who doesn’t quit, even when things get tough.
